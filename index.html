Teacher Guide

Lesson Briefing

Read this before starting. Do not show to the student.

A1 BeginnerMain Series Daily RoutinesSimple Present~55 min · 30 screens

Objective: The student will be able to describe daily routines using the simple present tense — including He/She + verb+S — and use 5 key vocabulary phrases naturally in speaking, anchored to a real video.

5 Key Words

  • wake up
  • hit snooze
  • make breakfast
  • head out
  • commute

Grammar targets

  • I + base verb
  • She/He + verb+S
  • doesn't + base verb
  • Do/Does questions

Wolf Method

  • Student speaks 80%
  • Always use starters
  • Infer grammar first
  • No yes/no questions

A1 Tips

  • Read starters aloud with student
  • Pause video at 1:20 + 3:10
  • Allow L1 for meaning
  • Praise every attempt

⚠ Before class

Pre-teach pronunciation of commute /kəˈmjuːt/ and routine /ruːˈtiːn/. Open the whiteboard (✏ button, top right) and write today's vocabulary before the student arrives.

Word: wake up

⏰
wake up
/weɪk ʌp/
Meaning

To stop sleeping and open your eyes. To become conscious in the morning and start your day.

Synonym

get up · rise · stir

Example

"I wake up at 7am every day."  |  "She wakes up at 8am." (+ S!)

❓ Quick check — which sentence is correct?

A"She wake up at 8am."
B"She wakes up at 8am." ⭐
C"She is wake up at 8am."

Vocabulary 2 of 5 · Screen 6

Word: hit snooze

💤
hit snooze
/hɪt snuːz/
Meaning

To press the snooze button on your alarm clock to get 5–10 more minutes of sleep before it rings again.

In the video

"I'm not a morning person, so sometimes I will hit snooze on my alarm clock."

Grammar note

I hit snooze. → She hits snooze. (She/He/It always gets +S!)

❓ Complete the sentence:

Sarah snooze every morning.

Then your teacher asks: "Is hitting snooze a good habit? Why / why not?"
Vocabulary 3 of 5 · Screen 7

Word: make breakfast

🍳
make breakfast
/meɪk ˈbrekfəst/
Meaning

To prepare your morning meal at home. In English, we say make breakfast — not "cook breakfast".

Important!
✅ I make breakfast ✅ She makes breakfast ❌ I cook breakfast
In the Video

"Now it's time to make breakfast. I don't like typical American food. I like eating fruits and veggies."

Speak Now
I make breakfast every morning. I usually eat ___.
I don't make breakfast. I usually eat ___ or I buy ___.
Teacher asks: "What do you eat for breakfast? Why do you like it?"
Vocabulary 4 of 5 · Screen 8

Word: head out

🚪
head out
/hɛd aʊt/
Meaning

To leave a place — usually your home. Very natural and informal in spoken English.

Synonym

leave · go out · set off · depart

Examples

"I head out at 8:45 every morning."
"She heads out the door." (+S with She!)

Common pattern

head out + at + time  |  head out + the door  |  head to + place

❓ Fill in:

She out at 8:45.
Speak Now
I head out at ___ every morning.
Before I head out, I always ___.
Vocabulary 5 of 5 · Screen 9

Word: commute

🚇
commute
/kəˈmjuːt/
Meaning

To travel regularly between your home and your workplace or school — every day or every week.

As a VERB

"I commute by bus."  |  "She commutes by subway." (+S!)

As a NOUN

"My commute takes 20 minutes."  |  "I have a long/short commute."

In the video

"I head out the door and commute to work. It takes about 15 minutes."

Speak Now
I commute to work / school by ___.
My commute takes ___ minutes.
Teacher asks: "Do you enjoy your commute? What do you do on the way?"

Transcript · Part 1 · Screen 14

Key Moments from the Video

Tap the yellow words to see grammar explanations.

⏱ 0:05 "I normally start my morning around 8am and I wake up to the sound of my alarm clock. I'm not a morning person, so sometimes I will hit snooze."
wake up — With I, use the BASE form: I wake up.
With She / He, add -S: She wakes up. ⭐ This is the Simple Present rule!
hit snooze — With I: I hit snooze. | With She: She hits snooze.
Notice: hit → hits — She always gets the -S!
⏱ 1:20 "After I wake up, I get out of bed and stretch. Then I head straight to the bathroom to wash my face and brush my teeth."
get out of bed → She gets out of bed (+S with She!)
This is a phrasal verb: get + out + of + bed. Very common in daily life!
head straight to = go directly, without stopping.
She heads straight to the bathroom. (heads = head + S)

Transcript · Part 2 · Screen 15

Transcript — Continued

Continue tapping the yellow words for grammar notes.

⏱ 2:40 "Now it's time to make breakfast. I normally don't like to eat a typical American breakfast. I like eating fruits and veggies or something from different cultures."
make breakfast — I make | She makes (+S).
⚠ We say MAKE breakfast, not ~~cook breakfast~~.
don't like — Negative with I: I don't like…
Negative with She: She doesn't like…
⭐ After doesn't — verb stays BASE form, no extra S!
⏱ 3:55 "After eating, I make the bed. I like starting the day with a clean room. Then I head out the door and commute to work. It takes about 15 minutes."
make the bed = arrange your sheets and pillows so the bed looks neat.
She makes the bed every morning. (makes = make + S)
head out = leave. Informal and very natural in spoken English.
I head out. → She heads out. (heads = head + S)
commute — as verb: She commutes by subway.
As noun: "Her commute takes 15 minutes."

Grammar Rule · Screen 17

Simple Present — The Rule

SubjectVerb formExample
Ibase verbI wake up at 8.
You / We / Theybase verbThey commute by bus.
He / She / It ⭐verb + SShe wakes up at 8.

⭐ Only He / She / It gets the -S. Always. No exceptions (except: have → has, go → goes, do → does)

Negatives: don't vs doesn't

SubjectNegativeExample
I / You / We / Theydon't + verbI don't eat meat.
He / She / Itdoesn't + verbShe doesn't eat meat.

⭐ After doesn't, the verb goes back to BASE form — no extra S!

Spelling rules for verb + S:

Ends in…RuleExample
Most verbs+ swakes · makes · likes
-ch / -sh / -o / -x+ eswatches · goes
consonant + yy → iesstudies · carries
have (irregular)→ hasShe has breakfast.

Conversation · Screen 23

A Real Conversation

Read this dialogue. Notice how daily routine language is used in a real conversation.

🧑
Hey! What time do you usually wake up?
👩
I wake up at 8am. But I always hit snooze first! 😅 I need at least 10 more minutes.
🧑
Ha! Me too. Do you eat breakfast before work?
👩
Yes! I make breakfast every morning. I don't like fast food. I prefer fruits and eggs. It only takes 10 minutes.
🧑
That sounds healthy! What time do you head out?
👩
Around 8:45. Before I leave, I always make the bed. I need a clean room when I come home!
🧑
Nice! How do you get to work? Do you drive?
👩
No, I commute by subway. My commute takes about 15 minutes. I listen to music on the way!
